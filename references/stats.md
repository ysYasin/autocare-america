## Shop costs

- Average car repair shop visit: **$386** (AAA, 2024)
- Average check engine light diagnosis fee: **$100–$165** at most independent shops; **$130–$220** at dealerships
- Catalytic converter replacement: **$400–$1,200** depending on vehicle and whether OEM or aftermarket
- Water pump replacement: **$300–$700** parts and labor
- Heater core replacement: **$800–$1,500** (labor-intensive; typically 6–10 hours at shop rates)
- Transmission rebuild: **$3,000–$8,000** depending on vehicle
- Head gasket repair: **$1,500–$2,500** on a 4-cylinder; **$3,000–$5,000** on a V8

## Service intervals

- Full-synthetic oil change: **7,500–10,000 miles** (modern engines)
- Conventional oil change: **3,000–5,000 miles**
- Engine air filter: **15,000–30,000 miles** (normal conditions); **12,000–15,000 miles** (dusty/dirt road driving)
- Tire rotation: **5,000–7,500 miles**
- Battery average lifespan: **3–5 years** (hot climates: **2–4 years**; cold climates: **4–6 years**)
- Serpentine belt (EPDM): **60,000–100,000 miles**
- Transmission fluid (normal driving): **60,000 miles**; (city driving): **40,000–50,000 miles**; (towing/stop-go): **30,000 miles**
- Coolant flush: every **5 years or 50,000 miles** in real-world use (OAT long-life)

## Technical specs

- Engine oil: operational temperature **195–220°F** on most engines; oil at full temp can reach **220°F**
- Cold-cranking amperage drops **35%** at **0°F** versus **77°F**
- Brake caliper pistons: front compress straight back; rear integrated parking brake pistons rotate clockwise ~**30–40 full turns** to retract
- Brake pad minimum: **2mm** friction material; wear sensors trigger at **2–3mm**
- Brake pad replacement interval: front **30,000–70,000 miles**; rear typically outlasts front by **20,000–30,000 miles**
- ATF oxidation at 80,000 miles: **4× baseline** oxidation level (Blackstone Labs)
- Engines switched to full-synthetic at 80,000 miles showed **18% lower iron content** in used-oil samples (Blackstone Labs fleet data)
- Full-synthetic ATF loses ~**30–40%** of friction modifier content by 60,000 miles
- Approximately **40%** of transmission rebuilds are on vehicles whose owners believed "lifetime fluid" means permanent (shop survey data)
