Bucket 3 — Off-page SEO

Off-page SEO — easier to tank than to help.

Off-page SEO is basically backlinks. Real gains take months of careful outreach — and one bad batch can undo a year of work. Skip it unless you really have to.
1
Outreach
Broken-link swap
Legit
Find a broken link on someone's site that used to point to a resource like yours. Email them: "your link is dead — here's mine."
How
SEMRush → Backlink Analytics → filter for broken links on sites in your niche.
2
Contribution
Guest posts
Legit
Write a real article for a site in your niche. Your byline or bio gets to link back to you. Slow, but safe.
How
Google "[your industry] + guest post". Pitch the ones with active blogs.
3
Earned media
HARO / Connectively
Legit · Free
Journalists post queries looking for expert sources. Reply fast, get quoted — and get a byline link from a real publication.
How
Subscribe to the daily emails. Answer queries in your niche within 1–2 hours. Keep pitches tight.
4
Paid placement
Quality paid links
Expert only
Real publishers. Real editorial. Actually works if you know who to pay. Blows up your site if you don't.
How
Vet the domain yourself. Check traffic, DR, and who else links from it. If it looks spammy, walk.
★
The real shortcut
Make something good enough that it earns links on its own.
This video is free, long, and well-researched — people will link to it because it's useful. That's the entire game. No outreach, no PBNs, no begging journalists.
!
The trap · this kills sites
Avoid PBNs & link farms at all costs.
Most cheap "SEO experts" on Upwork use Private Blog Networks — thousands of fake sites linking to each other. Google spots the pattern and tanks your rankings. Recovery takes months. Sometimes never.
Red flags
"$50 for 100 DR-50 backlinks"
"Instant links, no waiting"
"Ranks in 7 days guaranteed"
Bulk packages on Fiverr
My take: nail buckets 1 + 2, ignore bucket 3. On-page + technical SEO alone will rank you for 95% of niches. Backlinks come naturally when your content is actually good.
